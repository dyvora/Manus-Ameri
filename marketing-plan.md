# Manus+Ameri Marketing Plan

## 1. Executive Summary
Manus+Ameri is a non-partisan civic tracker that helps U.S. residents discover their federal representatives, explore legislative profiles, and monitor relevant news in one responsive web experience. This marketing plan outlines how to launch, grow, and sustain an engaged user base of civically curious citizens, advocates, journalists, and educators.

## 2. Objectives & KPIs
- **Launch Objectives (Months 0-3)**
  - Reach 15,000 unique visitors.
  - Convert 6% of visitors into registered users (900 sign-ups).
  - Achieve 40% repeat visit rate within 30 days.
- **Growth Objectives (Months 4-12)**
  - Grow organic traffic to 25,000 sessions/month.
  - Maintain cost-per-acquisition (CPA) under $4 for paid channels.
  - Secure 5 strategic partnerships with civic organizations or educational institutions.
- **Engagement Objectives**
  - Average session duration above 3 minutes.
  - Email newsletter open rate of 35% and click-through rate of 8%.
  - Collect at least 300 qualitative feedback responses in the first year to guide product iterations.

## 3. Target Audience & Personas
1. **Civic Explorer (Primary)**
   - Age 25-45, digitally savvy, moderate political engagement.
   - Needs a trusted source to learn about representatives and issues quickly.
   - Motivations: stay informed, vote responsibly, share knowledge with peers.
2. **Community Advocate**
   - Works or volunteers with civic groups, nonprofits, or grassroots campaigns.
   - Seeks tools to educate community members about government representation.
   - Motivations: mobilize audiences, advocate effectively, track legislative activity.
3. **Educator & Student**
   - High school or college instructors, debate coaches, political science students.
   - Requires accurate, accessible information and assignments for civic literacy.
   - Motivations: integrate current events with curriculum, encourage civic participation.
4. **Journalist & Researcher (Secondary)**
   - Local reporters or bloggers covering politics and elections.
   - Needs fast access to representative profiles and recent news coverage.

## 4. Value Proposition & Messaging
- **Core Promise:** "Instantly understand who represents you in Washington and what they are doing right now."
- **Key Differentiators:**
  - Unified dashboard combining representatives, committees, and relevant news.
  - Non-partisan tone and curated data from authoritative sources (Google Civic Info, ProPublica, News APIs).
  - Mobile-friendly, single-page experience with intuitive navigation.
- **Messaging Pillars:**
  1. **Clarity:** Up-to-date, digestible profiles of elected officials.
  2. **Empowerment:** Tools to take informed civic action, vote, or advocate.
  3. **Trust:** Transparent sourcing, no partisan bias, respectful of user privacy.
- **Brand Voice:** Informative, respectful, optimistic, and solution-oriented.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- **Direct Competitors:** GovTrack, Vote Smart, Ballotpedia (rich data but fragmented experiences).
- **Indirect Competitors:** News outlets, civic education platforms, activism apps (offer information but not personalized to the user).
- **Positioning:** Manus+Ameri stands out by combining personalization, real-time news, and an elegant UX purpose-built for everyday citizens.

## 6. Go-To-Market Strategy
### 6.1 Pre-Launch (Month -1 to Launch)
- Finalize brand identity, landing page, and product walkthrough demo.
- Set up analytics (GA4, Mixpanel), CRM (e.g., HubSpot starter), and marketing automation.
- Build an email waitlist via teaser site with value proposition and newsletter promise.
- Recruit beta testers from civic groups, universities, and social communities.
- Prepare a press kit: brand story, founder bios, screenshots, product fact sheet.

### 6.2 Launch (Month 0)
- Release product with a launch blog post and email announcement to waitlist.
- Publish a press release through PRWeb or EIN Presswire targeting civic tech outlets.
- Run a coordinated social media campaign (#KnowYourRep) on LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Instagram, TikTok.
- Host a live webinar/demo with Q&A covering how to use the app and future roadmap.
- Offer referral incentives (e.g., exclusive badges, early feature access) for sharing.

### 6.3 Post-Launch Growth (Months 1-12)
- Expand content marketing (weekly blog posts, data insights, explainers).
- Execute targeted paid campaigns during key political moments (State of the Union, elections).
- Develop partnerships with voter registration drives, libraries, educators, and civic podcasts.
- Launch in-product feedback loop and iterate on features based on usage data.

## 9. SEO & Discoverability
- Conduct keyword research around "find my representative", "federal representatives lookup", "representative news".
- Build pillar pages for "How to contact your senator" and "Understanding congressional committees" with internal linking to app features.
- Optimize metadata (title tags, alt text, schema markup for political representatives).
- Encourage backlinks through guest posts, partnerships, and data-driven reports (e.g., "Most-covered representatives in the news").
- Maintain fast load times and mobile performance to support Core Web Vitals.

## 10. Lifecycle & Retention Strategy
- **Onboarding:** Step-by-step tutorial highlighting search, profiles, and calendar features. Offer quick tips via modal or email drip.
- **Engagement:** Personalized news digests and committee updates based on saved representatives.
- **Reactivation:** Automated emails when major votes or breaking news occur for saved officials.
- **Advocacy:** Reward users who invite friends with premium features (e.g., custom alerts, saved articles).

## 11. Metrics & Analytics
- **Acquisition Metrics:** Traffic by channel, new accounts, conversion rate, CPA.
- **Engagement Metrics:** Time on site, page depth, feature usage (profile views, news tab toggles).
- **Retention Metrics:** 7-day and 30-day returning user cohorts, churn reasons from exit surveys.
- **Impact Metrics:** User-reported actions (contacting reps, attending events), partner satisfaction scores.
- Create dashboard in GA4/Looker Studio and schedule monthly reporting reviews with the team.

## 13. Timeline & Milestones

- **Month 0:** Public launch, press outreach, social campaign, live demo.
- **Month 1:** Release civic literacy content series, start retargeting ads.
- **Month 2:** Host advocacy webinar, onboard first partnership cohort.
- **Month 3:** Publish election readiness toolkit, evaluate KPIs, adjust messaging.
- **Month 4-6:** Scale SEO content, secure additional partnerships, introduce personalized alerts.
- **Month 6-12:** Expand nationwide media outreach, iterate features based on feedback, prepare for major election cycles.

## 14. Risk Mitigation
- **Data Accuracy:** Regularly validate API responses and provide disclaimers for placeholders.
- **Political Neutrality:** Establish editorial guidelines and fact-check processes to maintain non-partisan trust.
- **User Privacy:** Comply with privacy regulations, clearly state data usage, and offer opt-outs for email alerts.
- **Resource Constraints:** Prioritize high-ROI channels, repurpose content across mediums, leverage partnerships for reach.

## 15. Team & Responsibilities
- **Marketing Lead:** Owns strategy, analytics, and coordination with product.
- **Content Strategist:** Manages blog, newsletters, editorial calendar.
- **Community & Partnerships Manager:** Builds relationships with civic groups and educators.
- **Growth Marketer:** Runs paid campaigns, SEO initiatives, and conversion optimization.
- **Design Support (part-time/contract):** Produces visuals, infographics, and social assets.

## 16. Measurement & Iteration
Conduct quarterly reviews of KPIs, user feedback, and market trends. Adjust channel mix, messaging, and feature prioritization based on performance. Maintain open communication with product and engineering to align marketing insights with roadmap decisions.
